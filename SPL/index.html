Our Story

Samon Petroleum Limited “SPL”

Samon Petroleum Limited “SPL” is a Nigerian based integrated Oil and Gas company with strategic interests in the West African downstream value chain cutting across trading, storage, marketing and distribution of petroleum products.

SPL was conceived and founded by a group of oil traders that recognized the need in the energy trading market for a responsible commodity trading company. We’re focused on providing high-quality fuels in West Africa at competitive prices. Over the last twenty years, SPL has imported and traded mainly gasoil, gasoline, dpk/jet A1 and all associated services. SPL also owns a storage facility in Calabar, Nigeria which was built with Department of Petroleum Resources specifications and API.

The Company’s primary asset is its ownership and operatorship of a 60 million litres terminal in the major commercial hub in the Southern Region of Nigeria – Calabar Free Trade Zone, Cross River State. This terminal has storage capacity for Mogas, Gasoil, JET and DPK and provides the platform through which SPL takes its trading positions across various refined petroleum products.

In addition to the storage and trading business, the Company owns several retail outlets primarily located across the Eastern region of Nigeria. These outlets provide additional channels for the distribution of the various refined products.SPL is midway through the construction of a state-of-the-art jetty facility in the Calabar Free Trade Zone and are poised to commence the construction of an LPG storage terminal.

Samon Petroleum Limited depot is strategically located within the Free Trade Zone in Calabar, there are seventeen depots within the zone and nine outside the zone. What We Value

Health, Safety & Environment Policy (H.S.E Policy)

To increase our competitiveness and sustain our reputation, we are committed to the safe and efficient operation of our facilities. We have taken an uncompromising stand in the maintenance of high-quality standards in safety, security, health and environmental measures in all angles of our business and we demand same from our suppliers, contractors and other service providers. Our goal is to prevent all accidents, injuries and occupational illness and to protect the environment.

Below are just a few of the many safety measures taken:

  • Our employees are responsible for observing all of the safety and health plans that apply to their jobs.
  • A proactive process focusing on increased safety awareness has been put in place to help prevent accidents and injuries at our facilities.
  • Each of us must promptly report unsafe or unhealthy conditions and immediately take steps to correct those conditions.

Corporate Social Responsibility

SPL has continually supported the implementation of projects that are most relevant to our stakeholders and communities in which we live and work. SPL aims to supports quality education at the grassroots to the youth, school infrastructure development, capacity building and economic empowerment. We have developed a strategic relationship with the community leaders to assist/advance our reach.

Volunteering in the community: SPL and its employees volunteer and supports charitable organization and agencies in the local communities in form of foundation grants, sponsorship and corporate contributions. We are focus on giving to communities where the Company has employee’s presence.

Sustainability

At SPL, sustainability is at the heart of everything we do, to us it means not harming the environment or depleting natural resources and supporting long-term ecological balance.

We at SPL aim to operate a responsible, safe and profitable business which involves getting the basic right. We believe keeping sustainability as the consistent substratum of our approach to doing business is not only right for society, human rights but also great for SPL’s success.

SPL supports government agencies; environmental and community groups; and trade, professional and industry associations devoted to efforts that protect, conserve and sustain our environmental resources. These efforts may include clean-ups programs, educational programs, and research projects.

Liquefied Petroleum Gas “LPG” plant

SPL has acquired a land space also at the Trade Free Zone in Calabar State for our proposed LGP plant. The Nigeria market is one of the highest consumers of cooking gas and that’s the sector we plan to invest in.
